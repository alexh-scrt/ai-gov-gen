Overview

AI Gov Gen is a web-based tool that guides teams through a structured AI risk assessment questionnaire and automatically generates compliance-ready governance documents for AI tool deployments.

The tool draws on established frameworks including the HKS AI Risk Framework and NIST AI RMF to produce tailored governance checklists, System Security Plan (SSP) entries, and AI use policy documents. Outputs are available as formatted DOCX and PDF files suitable for submission to compliance reviewers or insertion into existing security documentation.

No data is persisted beyond your current browser session. Questionnaire responses and generated documents exist only in your session cookie and are cleared when you close your browser or reset the assessment.
{# --- How It Works --- #}

How It Works

  1. Select a compliance framework — choose the standard that best aligns with your organisation's regulatory obligations (NIST AI RMF, CMMC Level 2, Lloyd's Market, etc.).
  2. Complete the questionnaire — answer categorised questions about your AI system's data governance, model risk, operational security, and compliance posture.
  3. Review risk scores — the scoring engine maps your responses to Low / Medium / High / Critical risk levels per category and calculates an overall AI deployment risk score.
  4. Download governance documents — receive a governance checklist, SSP control entries, and a full AI use policy document, downloadable as DOCX or PDF.

Disclaimer

Important: AI Gov Gen generates governance documentation automatically based on questionnaire responses provided by the system owner. The generated documents are intended as a starting point for governance programmes and do not constitute legal, regulatory, or professional compliance advice. The organisation using these documents is solely responsible for ensuring that its AI systems comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and contractual obligations. All generated documents must be reviewed, validated, and approved by qualified compliance, legal, and/or security professionals before formal adoption or submission to any regulatory body or auditor.
